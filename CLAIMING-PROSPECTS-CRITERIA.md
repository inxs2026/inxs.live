## ✅ WHAT TO LOOK FOR (Horses Worth Claiming)

### 1. **SOUNDNESS FIRST**
- Racing **every 2-4 weeks** consistently
- Working **regularly between races** (every 7-14 days)
- **NO big gaps** (2+ months = red flag)
- Recent workout pattern clean and steady

### 2. **FORM & PERFORMANCE**
- **Steady or improving Beyers** (not declining)
- Recent **competitive finishes** (close 2nds, winning at level)
- Running **well at current class** (not being outrun badly)
- Recent close 2nd = still trying, wants to win

### 3. **VALUE ANGLES**
- **Dropping in class** with proven ability at higher level
- **Recent positive form change** (equipment, surface, distance)
- **Barn upgrade potential** (weak trainer, horse has talent)
- **Claimed recently and WINNING** (new connections found something)

---

## 🚫 RED FLAGS - AVOID THESE HORSES

### SOUNDNESS RED FLAGS
1. **Long layoffs** (2+ months between races)
2. **No/few workouts** = hiding soundness issue
3. **Sporadic work pattern** (not maintaining fitness)
4. **Geldings after long break** = often soundness surgery
5. **Just claimed and sitting** = new owner found a problem

### FORM RED FLAGS
6. **Beyer drops** (was 80, now 50 = decline)
7. **Declining performance** (past peak, fading)
8. **Being outrun badly** at current level
9. **Multiple trainer changes** (being passed around)

### THE BIG ONE: CONNECTIONS DUMPING
10. **Expensive purchase now cheap** = they know something is wrong
    - $125k yearling at $12.5k tag = **AVOID**
    - Good trainer dumping cheap = **MAJOR RED FLAG**
    - Elite connections willing to lose money = **RUN AWAY**

---

## WHY PURCHASE PRICE DOESN'T MATTER

**Bad Logic:** "They paid $125k, now $12.5k, what a bargain!"

**Reality:** Good trainers don't dump valuable horses. If D'Angelo paid $125k and is now risking losing it for $12.5k, that horse has:
- Soundness issues
- Doesn't train well
- Behavioral problems
- Doesn't run to pedigree
- Physical limitations

**The connections know the horse better than you. Trust their actions, not the pedigree page.**

## CLAIMING ANALYSIS CHECKLIST

Before recommending ANY horse as a claiming prospect:

- [ ] Check workout pattern (regular? gaps?)
- [ ] Check race spacing (every 2-4 weeks?)
- [ ] Check Beyer trend (improving/steady/declining?)
- [ ] Check recent form (competitive? trying?)
- [ ] Check trainer stats (good with claimers?)
- [ ] **RED FLAG CHECK:** Big purchase price at low tag? → AVOID
- [ ] **RED FLAG CHECK:** Long layoff? → AVOID
- [ ] **RED FLAG CHECK:** Few/no workouts? → AVOID
- [ ] **RED FLAG CHECK:** Beyers declining? → AVOID

## CLAIMING PROSPECTS vs BETTING PICKS

**THESE ARE DIFFERENT THINGS:**

**Betting pick:** Horse likely to win THIS race
- Can bet on unsound horses if they're fit TODAY
- Can bet on horses being dumped if price is right
- Short-term: just win this one race

**Claiming prospect:** Horse you want to OWN and race
- Must be sound for FUTURE races
- Must have upside or consistent ability
- Long-term: keep racing and earning

**Don't confuse the two!**
