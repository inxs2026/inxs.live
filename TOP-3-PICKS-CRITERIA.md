# TOP 3 PICKS - METHODOLOGY & CRITERIA
**Last updated:** February 14, 2026

## ⚠️ RULE: FOLLOW THIS METHODOLOGY FOR ALL RACE ANALYSIS

**Purpose:** Document the system that's been working well for top 3 picks

**Note:** This is for BETTING PICKS (win this race), not claiming prospects (own long-term)

---

## THE SYSTEM THAT WORKS

### 1. **CALCULATE AVERAGE BEYER (Last 3 Races)**
- Always include for every pick
- Shows current form level
- Compare to field average
- Format: "Avg Beyer (last 3): XX.X"

**Why it works:** Objective measure of recent performance, easy to compare across horses

---

### 2. **RECENT FORM ANALYSIS**
Look at last 3-5 races:
- **Finishes:** Close 2nds, 3rds better than distant 4ths, 5ths
- **Competitiveness:** Lost by a nose > lost by 10 lengths
- **Consistency:** 2-2-3 better than 1-9-9
- **Trajectory:** Improving (3-2-1) vs declining (1-4-8)

**Key phrases that work:**
- "Multiple close runner-up finishes show consistent competitiveness"
- "Recent winner with competitive figures"
- "Best speed figures in field"
- "Improving form, last two races show upward trend"

---

### 3. **CLASS ANALYSIS**
Where is the horse relative to today's class?

**Positive angles:**
- **Dropping:** Ran well at higher level, now dropping
- **Proven at level:** Won or placed multiple times at this class
- **Class edge:** Best recent figures vs today's competition

**Negative signals:**
- **Rising too fast:** Jump from $8k to $35k in one race
- **Outclassed last time:** Beaten badly at this level before
- **Never won at level:** Always struggles here

**Key phrases:**
- "Drops from MCL to straight claiming - class relief"
- "Proven winner at this level (2 wins in last 6 months)"
- "Best figure in field - ran 85 against today's 60s"

---

### 4. **TRAINER/JOCKEY ANGLES**
Use DRF trainer stats when available:

**Strong angles:**
- Trainer % for specific situation (2nd start, Lasix, blinkers, claims)
- "Joseph Jr barn excels with second-starters (25% wins)"
- "D'Angelo 22% with claimers, 21% with equipment changes"
- "Coy barn strong with routes (23% wins)"

**Jockey upgrade:**
- Elite jockey switching on = confidence
- "Castellano picks up mount" (from lesser rider)
- "Gaffalione aboard" (consistent quality)

**What to note:**
- Trainer hitting (18%+) or cold (<10%)
- Specific angle stats (first Lasix, blinkers on/off)
- Recent claims by trainer (shows confidence)

---

### 5. **WORKOUT PATTERNS**
Recent works show readiness:

**Positive signs:**
- **Bullet works:** "Sharp bullet work :48§ on 2/5"
- **Recent works:** Within 7 days of race
- **Series of works:** Steady pattern (every 5-7 days)
- **Speed:** Fast works for class level

**Include when:**
- First-time starters (only data available)
- Layoff horses (works show fitness)
- Recent bullet (shows sharpness)

**Format:**
- "Sharp recent works including bullet :35 on 1/17"
- "Works show readiness after layoff"
- "Regular work pattern indicates fitness"

---

### 6. **PACE SCENARIO**
How will the race unfold?

**Key concepts:**
- **Speed horses:** Like to lead, press early
- **Closers:** Come from behind
- **Pace advantages:** Lone speed (clear lead) or lone closer (slow pace)
- **Tactical position:** Post position matters for style

**Include when relevant:**
- "Lone speed in field - clear path to lead"
- "Slow pace suits closer style"
- "Draws rail for pressing tactics"
- "Post 10 allows stalking trip from outside"

---

### 7. **SURFACE/DISTANCE FIT**
Does today's conditions suit the horse?

**Breeding angles:**
- Turf pedigree on turf
- Dirt sprinter at 6F
- Distance breeding (stamina for routes)

**Past performance:**
- Won at this distance before
- Surface switch working (turf to dirt, synthetic to dirt)
- "Returns to preferred surface"

**Include when strong:**
- "Breeding suggests turf specialist (sire X, dam by Y)"
- "Best figures all at this 6F distance"
- "Surface switch to dirt suits speed style"

## RANKING THE TOP 3

### **#1 PICK (Top Selection)**
Should have MOST of these:
- Best/competitive Beyer average
- Recent positive form (wins, close 2nds)
- Class advantage or proven at level
- Strong trainer/jockey angle
- Favorable pace scenario OR recent works

**Don't pick #1 just because:**
- Highest odds (longshot)
- Only good workout
- Just one strong angle

### **#2 PICK (Runner-Up)**
Strong contender but missing 1-2 elements:
- Similar Beyers but less consistency
- Good class but no recent win
- Strong connections but question mark (distance, surface)
- Could win if #1 doesn't fire

### **#3 PICK (Third Choice)**
Viable but more question marks:
- Upside play (improving, first-time angle)
- Proven but inconsistent
- Class/speed advantage but form concerns
- Could hit the board, upset potential

## RACE TYPE SPECIFICS

### **MAIDEN CLAIMING**
- First-timers: Rely on works, breeding, trainer stats
- Experience: Value consistent placings
- Drops: MCL to straight claiming = positive

### **CLAIMING**
- Recent form most important
- Proven at price level
- Trainer claiming patterns matter
- Speed figures vs today's class

### **ALLOWANCE/NON-WINNERS**
- Class analysis key (N1X, N2X levels)
- Career trajectory matters
- Quality of competition in past races

### **STAKES**
- Grade/class very important
- Past stakes performance
- Best Beyers usually win
- Trainer stakes record matters

```markdown
[Brief description of why #1 pick: best angle + form + class/connections]

**2. HORSE NAME** - Avg Beyer (last 3): XX.X
[Why solid #2: strong points + what separates from #1]

**3. HORSE NAME** - Avg Beyer (last 3): XX.X  
[Why #3: upside/value angle + question marks to note]
```

**Keep it concise:**
- 2-3 sentences per pick
- Lead with strongest angle
- Note any concerns for #2/#3
- Always include Beyer average

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

### ❌ Don't:
- Pick based on name/jockey alone without form
- Ignore recent poor form because "due to win"
- Overlook class drops/raises
- Forget to calculate Beyer averages
- Pick all favorites (find value too)
- Skip pace analysis in speed-heavy races

### ✅ Do:
- Start with Beyers (objective baseline)
- Weight recent form heavily (last 2-3 races)
- Note class relative to today
- Include trainer angles when strong
- Consider pace scenario
- Pick horses you can explain in 2-3 sentences
