### Speed & Beyer Analysis
**Key insight:** Need to calculate actual finishing times for beaten horses.

**Conversion ratio:** 2.5 lengths = 1 second (universal across sprints and routes)

**Example:**
- Winner: 59.00 seconds
- Horse finished 3rd, beaten by 2.5 lengths
- Horse's actual time: 59.00 + 1.00 = 1:00.00

**Beyer Speed Figures:**
- Each horse earns its own Beyer for that race
- Winner beaten me by 2.5 lengths → Winner's Beyer is ~2.5 points higher than mine
- Can compare Beyers directly across different horses in same race

**Use both for validation:**
- Time analysis: Shows raw speed (adjusted for beaten lengths)
- Beyer figures: Normalizes for track conditions, distance, surface
- Cross-check: Does the time difference match the Beyer difference?

**Field size = context, not critical:**
- 2nd in 4-horse field = beat only 2 horses (less impressive)
- 2nd in 12-horse field = beat 10 horses (more impressive)
- Worth noting, but not a make-or-break factor

### Class Drop Analysis
**Primary focus:** Where did horse finish at what class level vs today's class?

**ATTRACTIVE class drop:**
- Running 4th-5th at higher class level (competitive but not winning)
- Occasionally hitting the board (shows ability)
- Sound indicators present
- NOW dropping in class
- Why it works: Horse holding its own at tougher level, now gets easier competition

**PASS - Stagnant dropper:**
- Horse runs 6th-7th at $25k claiming
- Drops to $20k → still runs 6th-7th
- Drops to $16k → still runs 6th-7th
- Not declining, but not improving despite easier competition
- Red flag: Horse is consistently mediocre, has found its ceiling

**PASS - Gradual decline:**
- Slowly declining form (6th → 7th → 8th)
- Dropping repeatedly but still not improving
- Horse is getting worse, not just seeking easier spot

**The key question:** WHY is this horse dropping?
- Strategic drop = Trainer sees opportunity (attractive)
- Desperation drop = Horse declining, trainer searching (pass)

### Confidence & Trainer Strategy
**Carlo's insight:** Horses need confidence boosts, just like humans.

**Gradual decline = confidence erosion:**
- Horse keeps losing at $25k, $20k, $16k, $12.5k
- Getting beat up repeatedly
- Never gets the "win feeling" back
- Becomes a perpetual loser

**Strategic big drop = confidence rebuild:**
- Horse struggling at $25k
- Trainer makes ONE significant drop to $12.5k (risky - might get claimed)
- Horse dominates or runs well → confidence boost
- Even if claimed, horse is mentally reset
- If NOT claimed, trainer can move back up with a confident horse

**What to look for:**
- FIRST big drop after struggling = confidence reset attempt (ATTRACTIVE)
- Gradual drop pattern = no confidence opportunity given (PASS)
- Just won after big drop = confidence rebuilt, might move back up

**Trainer psychology angle:**
- Smart trainers rebuild horses with strategic big drops
- Lazy trainers just keep dropping gradually and hoping
- Trainer willing to risk claim to rebuild horse = sign of good horseman
