## Thoroughbred Age & Birth Month (11:48 AM - 12:10 PM)

### Universal Birthday Rule
**All thoroughbreds age on January 1st** regardless of actual birth month.

**Breeding season:**
- Restricted/regulated (not year-round)
- **Earliest foals:** January (prime breeding target)
- **May foals:** Considered LATE
- **June foals:** Rare but possible

### Development & Debut Timing

**Physical maturity varies:**
- Not just birth month - individual development matters
- January foal might develop slower than May foal
- Some horses just need more time

**Racing strategy:**
- **Well-developed horses** → Often race as 2yos
- **Late/less developed** → Skip 2yo season, debut as 3yos
- Not a red flag - can be smart horsemanship

### 3yo First-Time Starter Evaluation

**What to check (DATA POINTS, not red flags):**
1. **Birth month** - shown on DRF as "(Jan)", "(Mar)", "(May)" etc.
2. **Workout pattern** - Regular? Gaps? Recent bullets?
3. **Debut timing** - When vs track opening/season start
4. **Gaps in training** - Context matters (why the delay?)

**Carlo's opinion (not rule):**
- January/February foals = MAY be stronger (more mature, ready earlier)
- But late foals OR slower developers can be just as good
- Debuting early in season SLIGHTLY preferred for Jan/Feb foals

**Example scenarios:**
```
Track opens: April (Woodbine)
Horse turned 3: January 1

Debuts: May (1 month after opening)
- January foal, regular works since March → Likely ready
- May foal, works started April → Makes sense, late foal

Debuts: August (4 months after opening)  
- February foal, big workout gaps → Why the delay? Check pattern
- June foal, works started July → Late foal, needed time
```

**Critical: NONE of these are elimination criteria.**

All factors = pieces of the puzzle for comprehensive evaluation.  
Birth month + debut timing + works = complete picture.  
Don't disqualify based on timing alone.
