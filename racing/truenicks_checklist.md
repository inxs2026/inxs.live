# TrueNicks Quick-Reference Checklist

**Website:** https://www.truenicks.com/

---

## Step-by-Step: Running a TrueNicks Report

### 1. Gather Horse Information

Before going to TrueNicks, collect:

- [ ] **Horse's registered name** (easiest method)
  - OR if not yet named:
- [ ] **Sire (father) name**
- [ ] **Dam (mother) name**

**Where to find:**
- Racing programs
- Sales catalogs
- Equibase.com
- Horse's past performance records

---

### 2. Choose Report Type

**Recommended:** Enhanced Report (best value for prospect evaluation)

**Includes:**
- ✅ TrueNicks letter grade (A++ to D)
- ✅ Surface performance statistics (dirt vs turf)
- ✅ Top 15 racehorses bred the same way
- ✅ Racing statistics from the cross
- ✅ Auction results data
- ✅ Starters and success rates

**Alternative:** Classic Report (cheaper, less data)
- 5-generation pedigree
- Top 5 horses from cross
- Inbreeding marks

---

### 3. Run the Report

1. Go to https://www.truenicks.com/
2. Select **Enhanced Report**
3. Enter horse name OR sire + dam names
4. Submit and review results

---

### 4. Evaluate the Results

#### A. Nick Rating (Primary Score)

- [ ] **A++ or A+** → Exceptional genetics, strong foundation ✅
- [ ] **A or B+** → Good to very good cross, solid prospect ✅
- [ ] **B** → Average cross, proceed with caution ⚠️
- [ ] **C or D** → Below average to poor, likely avoid ❌

#### B. Surface Performance Statistics

Check historical data for this cross:

- [ ] **Dirt stats:** What % win on dirt? Earnings on dirt?
- [ ] **Turf stats:** What % win on turf? Earnings on turf?
- [ ] **Clear preference?** Does one surface dominate?

**Key Question:** Does this match the surfaces available to you?

#### C. Top Racehorses from Cross

Review the top 15 horses bred the same way:

- [ ] Did they race on dirt or turf mostly?
- [ ] What class level? (Stakes, allowance, claiming?)
- [ ] Any Grade 1 winners? (elite genetic potential)
- [ ] Consistent quality or just a few outliers?

#### D. Inbreeding Concerns

- [ ] Any significant inbreeding marked?
- [ ] If yes: Check "Enhanced Inbreeding Report" for risk level
- [ ] Too much inbreeding = genetic risks (fragility, health issues)

---

### 5. Cross-Reference with Prospect Data

Now compare TrueNicks data with your prospect evaluation:

#### Scenario A: Good Nick + Good Performance + Right Surface
- **Nick rating:** A+ or better ✅
- **Racing record:** Sound, consistent ✅
- **Surface match:** Currently racing on preferred surface ✅
- **Decision:** SOLID PROSPECT — buy if price is right

#### Scenario B: Good Nick + Poor Performance + WRONG Surface
- **Nick rating:** A+ or better ✅
- **Racing record:** Sound fundamentals (consistent starts, good workouts) ✅
- **Surface MISMATCH:** Racing dirt but cross favors turf (or vice versa) 💰
- **Decision:** VALUE OPPORTUNITY — buy cheap, switch surface, profit!

#### Scenario C: Good Nick + Soundness Issues
- **Nick rating:** A+ or better ✅
- **Racing record:** Missing seasons, irregular starts ❌
- **Decision:** RISKY — good genes, bad body. Proceed with extreme caution.

#### Scenario D: Poor Nick + Poor Performance
- **Nick rating:** C or D ❌
- **Racing record:** Mediocre or poor ❌
- **Decision:** AVOID — bad genetics confirmed by bad results

#### Scenario E: Poor Nick + Good Performance
- **Nick rating:** C or D ❌
- **Racing record:** Surprisingly good ✅
- **Decision:** ANOMALY — horse is overperforming genetics. Could regress. Caution.

---

### 6. Make Final Decision

Combine TrueNicks data with full evaluation:

**Checklist Integration:**

1. ✅ **Soundness:** No missing seasons, consistent racing
2. ✅ **Financials:** Good earnings per start
3. ✅ **Workouts:** Regular schedule, competitive times
4. ✅ **Nick Rating:** A+ or better
5. ✅ **Surface Match:** Either currently on right surface OR opportunity to switch

**If 4-5 boxes checked:** Strong prospect, move forward

**If 2-3 boxes checked:** Marginal prospect, evaluate risk/reward

**If 0-1 boxes checked:** Pass, too many red flags

## Pro Tips

💡 **For 2YOs with no races:** Nick rating becomes PRIMARY evaluation metric
- A++ = strong buy candidate
- A+/A = good candidate
- B or below = need other strong positives

💡 **Surface arbitrage play:** Look for A+ nicks on wrong surface with solid fundamentals

💡 **Compare siblings:** If prospect has siblings, check their TrueNicks and performance

💡 **Watch for "A++ but underperforming":** These are hidden gems if fundamentals are sound

💡 **Inbreeding isn't always bad:** Some successful patterns exist, check Enhanced Inbreeding Report
